SustainCycle

Transforming plastic waste into sustainable products while empowering rural women.

About Us

SustainCycle collects single-use plastic waste—like carry bags and food wrappers—from households, businesses, and NGOs. Without using electricity, heat, or chemicals, we convert cleaned, dried plastic into colorful threads using traditional spindles (charkha) and handlooms. These fabrics are then crafted into everyday products such as bags, pouches, wallets, and stationery.

Our Upcycling Process

  • Collecting Waste: We partner with NGOs, corporate waste producers, and eco-conscious individuals to source plastic waste.
  • Cleaning & Sorting: Waste is washed, sanitized, sun-dried, and then sorted by color and type without using chemicals.
  • Spinning & Weaving: Strips are spun on charkhas and woven on handlooms into vibrant fabric by rural artisans, mostly women.
  • Design & Production: The fabric is cut and stitched into market-ready products—bags, accessories, home décor, and stationery.

Why It Matters

  • Environmental: Redirecting plastic from landfills and oceans, promoting responsible consumption.
  • Social: Enabling stable jobs and financial independence for rural women and youth, who earn fair wages and gain confidence. Many had no prior employment.

Products reach local and international markets, and through CSR partnerships, we continuously scale our reach.
